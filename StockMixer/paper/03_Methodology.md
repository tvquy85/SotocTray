## 3. Methodology

### 3.1. Problem Formulation
Consider a financial market with $N$ liquid assets. At each time step $t$, the market environment provides a feature tensor $X_t \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times T \times F}$, where $T$ is the lookback window length and $F$ denotes the number of features (e.g., OHLCV prices, technical indicators). The goal of the portfolio optimization agent is to generate a portfolio weight vector $w_t \in \mathbb{R}^N$ such that $\sum_{i=1}^N w_{t,i} = 1$ and $w_{t,i} \ge 0$, which dictates the proportion of total capital allocated to each asset.

Let $r_t \in \mathbb{R}^N$ denote the actual forward return of the assets at step $t+1$. The gross return of the portfolio is $R_g = \sum_{i=1}^N w_{t,i} r_{t,i}$. Between step $t-1$ and $t$, the portfolio must be rebalanced from the drifted weights $w_{t-1}'$ to the new target weights $w_t$. This rebalancing incurs a transaction cost rate $c$, leading to the turnover penalty $C_t = c \sum_{i=1}^N |w_{t,i} - w_{t-1,i}'|$.

### 3.2. TC-StockMixer Backbone
Our framework builds upon the MLP-based StockMixer architecture, an efficient spatio-temporal mixer designed to aggregate financial signals without the computational bottleneck of self-attention. The backbone consists of two primary modules:
1. **Time-series Mixer:** Extracts temporal dependencies across the $T$ lookback steps using a series of Tri-U layers and Layer Normalization.
2. **Cross-Asset Mixer:** Aggregates spatial dependencies among the $N$ stocks, capturing sector-wide momentum and co-movement.

The raw output of the mixer is a score vector $S_t \in \mathbb{R}^N$, which is traditionally mapped to the target weights via a softmax function: $w_t = \text{Softmax}(S_t / \tau)$, where $\tau$ is a temperature hyperparameter.

### 3.3. Context-Conditioned Dynamic Penalty Network (ContextNet)
The fatal flaw of optimizing $w_t$ using a static transaction cost penalty is its blindness to the macro market regime. In highly volatile or sideways markets, a static penalty often allows the model to chase false breakouts, leading to severe capital erosion due to turnover. Conversely, during massive bear markets, static models fail to recognize the necessity of extreme risk aversion.

To solve this, we introduce `ContextNet`, a meta-learning module acting as the brain of the regime-aware optimization. `ContextNet` distills the global market state vector $M_t = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N X_{t,i} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times F}$ into two dynamic parameters:
$$ [\alpha_t, \lambda_t] = \text{ContextNet}(M_t) $$

1. **Transaction Cost Modulator ($\alpha_t \in [0.1, 2.0]$):** Scales the turnover penalty. When $M_t$ indicates high noise and low predictability, `ContextNet` surges $\alpha_t$ to restrict trading and enforce a "wait-and-see" approach.
2. **Downside Risk Aversion ($\lambda_t \in [0.0, 2.0]$):** Scales the downside variance penalty. When $M_t$ foreshadows a market crash, $\lambda_t$ aggressively penalizes the selection of assets with severe downside potential.

### 3.4. Unified Regime-Aware Loss Function
To train the backbone and `ContextNet` end-to-end, we define a unified differentiable loss function. The total loss $L$ combines spatial ranking prediction ($L_{rank}$), magnitude regression ($L_{reg}$), and the regime-aware portfolio objective ($L_{net}$):
$$ L = \beta L_{reg} + \gamma L_{rank} + \eta L_{net} $$

The regime-aware portfolio loss $L_{net}$ is the cornerstone of our framework, formulated to maximize net returns while strictly adhering to the dynamic constraints generated by `ContextNet`:
$$ L_{net} = - \left( R_g - c \cdot (\alpha_t \cdot \sum_{i=1}^N |w_{t,i} - w_{t-1,i}|) - \lambda_t \cdot \sum_{i=1}^N w_{t,i} (\min(r_{t,i}, 0))^2 \right) + \rho \sum_{i=1}^N w_{t,i}^2 $$

Where:
- $R_g$ is the gross expected return.
- The second term is the **Context-Conditioned Turnover Penalty**.
- The third term is the **Context-Conditioned Downside Variance Penalty**, heavily suppressing the allocation $w_{t,i}$ for assets that historically or predictably exhibit severe negative returns during the recognized market regime.
- The final term represents the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) concentration penalty to enforce diversification.
